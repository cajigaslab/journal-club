# Learnable latent embeddings for joint behavioural and neural analysis (CEBRA)
**Date:** 2025-08-13
**Presenter:** Bowen Wang
**URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06031-6
**Journal:** Nature

## Summary
CEBRA is a nonlinear dimensionality reduction method that generates consistent, interpretable latent spaces from high-dimensional neural recordings by combining contrastive learning with behavioral or temporal labels. Works in supervised, self-supervised, or hybrid modes.

## Key Points
- Outperforms conv-pi-VAE, autoLFADS, t-SNE, and UMAP in embedding consistency
- ~5 cm median position decoding error on hippocampal data
- R² = 88% decoding of hand trajectories in primate S1
- >95% accurate decoding of natural video frames in mouse visual cortex
- Scales to multi-session and multi-animal training
